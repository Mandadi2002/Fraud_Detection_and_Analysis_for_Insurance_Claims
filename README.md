# Abstract

Motor insurance fraud has become a major challenge for insurance companies across the world. Fraudulent claims result in significant financial losses for insurers and increase the overall cost of claim management. These losses often lead to higher insurance premiums, which negatively affect honest policyholders. In addition, fraudulent activities create operational challenges by making the claim verification process more complex and time-consuming. This can slow down the processing of legitimate claims and reduce the overall efficiency of insurance services. Traditional fraud detection methods mainly rely on manual investigation and rule-based systems, which are often unable to detect complex and evolving fraud patterns.
This project proposes a machine learning–based approach to effectively detect and analyze fraudulent motor insurance claims. The study uses historical insurance claim data that includes customer demographics, vehicle details, policy information, and claim-related characteristics. By analyzing these features, machine learning models can identify suspicious patterns and classify claims as either fraudulent or genuine.
Several supervised machine learning algorithms are implemented in this study, including Logistic Regression, Decision Tree, Random Forest, and XGBoost. These algorithms help in building predictive models that can automatically detect potential fraud cases. To improve model performance and reliability, ensemble learning techniques are also applied. Since fraud datasets are usually imbalanced, preprocessing techniques such as SMOTE (Synthetic Minority Over-sampling Technique) and class weighting are used to balance the data and enhance model accuracy.
The models are evaluated using performance metrics such as Precision, Recall, F1-Score, and ROC-AUC. Overall, the proposed approach helps insurance companies reduce fraud losses, improve claim verification, and support more efficient insurance operations.
